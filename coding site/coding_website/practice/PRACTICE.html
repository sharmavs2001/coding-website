Programming is about being able to solve a programming problem whose solution is a program. In order to do this, you must develop your problem-solving skills by exposing yourself to varied kinds of problems and applications. This requires practice. It's much like learning to play piano, or learning to play chess, or even learning to speak a foreign language. It's all about practice. In programming, abstract thinking is vital. How do you train your brain to think abstractly and solve any kind of programming problem? It's not something that you can learn in a classroom or from a book. It's not a conceptual entity; it's a way of thinking. Training your brain requires practice.
